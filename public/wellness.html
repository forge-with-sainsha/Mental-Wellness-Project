Wellness Tips & Activities

Evidence-based strategies to support your mental well-being.

Low StressMaintaining Your Well-being
Gratitude Journaling

Write 3 things you're grateful for each morning. A consistent practice reinforces a positive mindset.

Regular Exercise

Light exercise like walking, cycling, or yoga at least 3 times per week boosts your mood and energy.

Social Connection

Nurture relationships with friends and family. Schedule regular check-ins to stay connected.

Goal Setting

Set small, achievable goals each week to maintain motivation and a sense of progress.

Quality Sleep

Aim for 7–8 hours of consistent sleep each night to maintain emotional resilience.

Moderate StressStress Reduction Strategies
4-7-8 Breathing

Inhale 4s → hold 7s → exhale 8s. Repeat 4 times. Activates your body's relaxation response instantly.

Pomodoro Technique

Study for 25 minutes then take a 5-minute break. Keeps focus high and prevents mental burnout.

Digital Detox

Limit social media and screens to under 2 hours daily. Try app timers to enforce this boundary.

Talk It Out

Share how you're feeling with a trusted friend, family member, or student counselor. You don't have to go it alone.

Mindfulness Meditation

10 minutes of daily meditation using free apps like Insight Timer or Smiling Mind reduces cortisol levels.

Physical Activity

At least 30 minutes of physical activity, 3 times a week. Even a brisk walk significantly lowers stress hormones.

High StressAdvanced Coping & Support
Seek Professional Support

Contact a school counselor, therapist, or mental health professional. Taking this step is a sign of strength.

Student Support Services

Your institution may offer academic accommodations — deadline extensions, reduced load — to help during tough times.

Progressive Muscle Relaxation

Tense and release each muscle group from feet to forehead. 10 minutes daily significantly reduces physical tension.

Break Tasks Down

Break overwhelming tasks into the smallest possible steps. Focus on completing just one step at a time.

Crisis Support

If you feel overwhelmed or in crisis, reach out to a crisis helpline immediately. You matter and help is available.

Peer Support Groups

Connect with peer support groups on campus or online. Shared experiences reduce isolation and build resilience.
